David Tao Biography

The Rise of a Mandopop R&B Pioneer

David Tao (Tao Xuzhong) was born on July 11, 1969, in Hong Kong. He spent his childhood in Taipei and moved to California at age 15. His father, Tao Dawei, was a celebrated entertainer across film, TV, and music, and his mother, Wang Fulong, was a Peking opera performer. Immersed in both Eastern and Western cultures, he was destined for a unique musical path.

He graduated from UCLA, majoring in psychology with a minor in film. After school he worked in a civilian role at the Los Angeles Police Department, but his passion for music never faded.

Early Behind-the-Scenes Career (1993–1996)

In 1993, he met acclaimed producer Wang Zhiping at a record store in Los Angeles. Wang invited him to return to Taiwan, where David Tao began his production career. He quickly built a reputation by writing and producing for artists such as Jeff Chang, Sarah Chen, Winnie Hsin, Ronald Cheng, and Sun Yaowei.

Breakthrough and Impact (1997–2000)

In 1997, David Tao stepped into the spotlight with his self-titled album David Tao. It fused authentic American R&B with Mandopop and shocked the industry with its fresh sound. Classic tracks like “Airport at 10:30,” “Beach,” and “Simple Love” remain timeless. The album earned him the 9th Golden Melody Awards for Best New Artist and Best Album Producer.

In 1999, he released his second album I'm OK, which expanded his palette with more rock elements. Songs like “Find Yourself,” “Small Town Girl,” and “Just a Friend” became widely loved. He won Best Album Producer at the 11th Golden Melody Awards.

Black Tangerine and Social Reflection (2002)

In 2002, he released Black Tangerine, widely regarded as his artistic peak. The album explored the post‑9/11 world, social issues, and the darker side of human nature. The title track delivered a heavy rock punch, while “Angel” and “Twenty‑Two” highlighted his softer side.

Trilogy Finale and Beyond (2005–Present)

He released The Great Era (2005), So Beautiful (2006), and the rock‑leaning 69 Chapter (2009). In 2010, 69 Chapter earned him Best Mandarin Male Singer at the 21st Golden Melody Awards, completing his transformation from producer to star.

In recent years, he has remained active through tours and music programs, sharing his artistic vision and mentoring younger musicians.

Key Achievements

  • 9th Golden Melody Awards — Best New Artist, Best Album Producer
  • 11th Golden Melody Awards — Best Album Producer
  • 21st Golden Melody Awards — Best Mandarin Male Singer
  • Widely known as the “Godfather of Mandopop R&B”
